Glossary Of Terms

 

BIMBO                        

Not what you think! It’s a combination of an MBO and MBI.  

Business or Shares Worth

There are four main methods used to determine an indicative valuation.  

Price earnings: this method involves assessing future maintainable after tax earnings using a price earnings ratio that would fit the risk profile of a willing buyer / seller.  

Discounted cash flow:  this method calculates the vale of the business by discounting the future cash flows by an appropriate discount rate.  

Asset-based:  this method values the assets of the business based on the fair or open market value.  

Dividend yield:  this method calculated the value of the business by assessing the future dividend stream and a dividend yield which would fit the risk profile of a willing buyer/seller.  

Cash out                     

A cash out (also known as an “equity release”) is where a vendor disposes of his equity in phases. At the agreed stage, the vendor will release equity to the purchaser and simultaneously the purchaser will make payments to the vendor.

Corporate Finance

In a nutshell, corporate finance focuses on a business’s strategic planning issues.   

                                            

Deferred consideration

Deferred consideration is when a vendor defers all or part of his proceeds from the sale of his business and agrees to receive the consideration from the purchaser at a later date.  

Due diligence             

Due diligence is an investigation into financial, commercial and legal processes.  

Earn Out                     

An earn out is a type of deferred consideration that is only payable by the purchaser to the vendor if agreed performance benchmarks are achieved or exceeded in the future.  

EBITDA, EBITD, EBIT

Financial terms used to report a company's earnings as they would be without specific deductions taken from the total amount. The acronyms mean, respectively: earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, earnings before interest, taxes, and depreciation, and earnings before interest and taxes. EBITDA is often used to compare the profit potential between companies because it allows an "apples-to-apples" comparison. Expert’s caution, however, that EBITDA does not accurately reflect a company's ability to generate cash and should not be used interchangeably with the term "cash flow".  

Equity release             

Refer to ‘Cash Out’  

Funds for my business

There are a number of ways to raise funds: venture capitalists, banks, and business angels.  

Flotation                     

Refer to ‘IPO’  

Indemnity                   

An indemnity is a contractual promise by one party to make good the loss suffered by another party or to pay a defined sum to that other party.  

Intangible assets

Items such as goodwill, patents, copyrights, trademarks or research and development, which have no tangible existence.  

IPO                             

An Initial Public Offering of shares, this is the first occasion that a company offers shares for sales in a public market.  

Loan note                   

A form of vendor finance or deferred payment, in which the purchaser acts as a borrower, agreeing to make payments to the holder of the transferable loan note at a specified future date.  

MBI                             

MBI refers to a management by in, where an external management team buys out the existing owners of the business.  

MBO                            

MBO refers to a management buyout, where the incumbent management team buys out the existing owners of the business.  

Net assets                   

The value of total assets less total liabilities.  

Net worth                    

Total Capital & Reserves less intangible assets.  

PBIT                            

Profit Before Interest and Tax.  

PBT                             

Profit Before Tax.    

Ratchet                       

A ratchet is a way of increasing or decreasing a private equity houses or venture capitalist’s shareholding based on the non-achievement or achievement of agreed targets.    

Strategic Planning

Strategic planning is about the big picture: where the business is currently.    

Warranty                     

A warranty is an express or implied statement of fact made by a seller in a contract, confirming that a particular state of affairs exists.
